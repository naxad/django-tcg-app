About ShinyVerse

We’re collectors and hobby lovers on a mission to make buying, selling, and grading trading cards fast, fair, and fun—with zero guesswork.

ShinyVerse started as an idea by collectors who wanted a marketplace where condition, pricing, and trust come first. We obsess over the details—clear photos, honest descriptions, and a support team that actually answers. Our upcoming AI Grader combines computer vision with curated datasets to estimate sub-scores like centering, corners, edges, surface, and print quality, and to generate transparent, human-readable grading notes.

Whether you’re chasing a grail SAR or listing your binder finds, we want the process to be simple, protected, and rewarding. If you have feedback or want to partner, drop us a line at info@shinycards.eu.

Trust & Safety

  • Anti-fraud by default: evidence-based claims (unboxing video, packing verification) to keep things fair.

Our Story

We began as collectors trading locally, then selling online, and eventually building tools we wished existed: consistent and easy to use grading tools. As demand grew, we formalized ShinyVerse P.C. and focused on two pillars: great marketplace UX and explainable AI grading. The goal isn’t to replace human expertise—it’s to make it easier for everyone to buy and sell with confidence.

Today, our roadmap reflects what the community asks for most: fast listing, reliable grading support, and strong buyer protections. We iterate openly and ship improvements weekly.

How Our AI Grader Helps

Our AI models analyze uploaded photos to estimate sub-scores for centering, corners, edges, and surface. We combine model outputs with rule-based checks (e.g., border alignment tolerances) to produce a transparent assessment. Results include a numeric estimate, confidence range, and short notes (e.g., “micro whitening top-right; faint print line”).

Important: AI grades are advisory and may differ from third-party graders (PSA/CGC/Beckett). They’re designed to help you make informed decisions and create consistent listings—not to guarantee a specific slab grade.
